% 1. Abstract
% Using \chapter* keeps it unnumbered but we add it to TOC manually
\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}

\textbf{Atrial fibrillation (AF)} is the most common sustained cardiac arrhythmia, driven by regions of complex electrical activity within the atria. \textbf{Intracardiac electrograms (EGMs)} recorded during \textbf{ablation procedures} provide a direct window into these dynamics, yet their interpretation remains challenging due to the high spatial and temporal variability of the signals.

The goal of this project is to apply \textbf{machine learning} and \textbf{deep learning-based forecasting} techniques to predict the temporal evolution of EGM signals. The working hypothesis is that signals that are \textbf{easier to predict} correspond to \textbf{passively activated regions}, while those that are \textbf{harder to forecast} reflect \textbf{active arrhythmic drivers}.

This approach may help identify critical \textbf{ablation sites} and advance more personalised treatment strategies in atrial fibrillation.
